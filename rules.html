Rules & Guidelines

Eligibility

  • 01Works must have been first published (or released in paperback for the first time) within the current award year — January 1 to December 31.
  • 02Books must be written in English or have been translated into English and published by an English-language publisher.
  • 03Self-published works are eligible provided they have an ISBN and are available through at least one major retail or library channel.
  • 04Authors may submit to only one category per title. If a title is deemed more appropriate for a different category by the judging panel, the panel may re-categorize at their discretion.
  • 05Previously nominated but non-winning titles may be resubmitted once, in a subsequent year, if a new edition is released.

Submission Process

  • 01All submissions must be made by the author, publisher, or an authorized representative. Submissions open October 1 and close December 15 each year.
  • 02Submitting parties must provide two physical copies of the work to our mailing address and one digital copy (PDF or EPUB) via email.
  • 03A completed submission form must accompany each entry, including the category, publication date, publisher name, and a short synopsis.
  • 04There is no submission fee. The Socratic Awards is committed to remaining accessible to all publishers and authors.

Judging Criteria

  • 01Works are evaluated on literary merit, originality, quality of prose, structural coherence, and overall impact on the reader.
  • 02Commercial success, publisher prestige, and author reputation play no role in the judging process. All submissions are read without knowledge of sales figures.
  • 03Judges must recuse themselves from evaluating any work with which they have a personal or professional conflict of interest.
  • 04All deliberations are confidential. Individual judges' scores and comments will not be disclosed.

Announcements

  • 01Longlists are announced in January. Shortlists are announced in March. Winners are revealed in May.
  • 02All submitting parties will be notified by email at each stage of the process.
  • 03The decisions of the judging panel are final and not subject to appeal.
